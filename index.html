About Me

Hello, my name is Waleed Ehab McLean. I am currently a full-stack developer student in the UNC Coding Bootcamp. I began coding in August of 2020, learning different styles and languages. I chose to learn how to code for a myriad of reasons, mainly to change careers, learn new skills that will help me later on in life, and also to potentially teach others how to code.

I was born in Lumberton, North Carolina on February 24, 1986, and grew up in Saint Pauls, North Carolina. In 2004, I graduated from Saint Pauls High School in Saint Pauls, North Carolina, enrolling at Winston-Salem State University shortly after. Unfortunately I had to withdraw from the university in 2006 for personal issues. In 2007, I began working for the Goodyear Tire and Rubber Company until I resigned in 2020 to take the UNC Bootcamp.

Although I withdrew from WSSU, I re-enrolled to the school in 2018, switching my concentration from History to Finance and Computer Science. I continued going to the school until this past year when I decided to not go to school for the Fall 2019/Spring 2020 semester. The reason for this was because of financial issues (I was paying for all school expenses myself) and the COVID-19 pandemic.
